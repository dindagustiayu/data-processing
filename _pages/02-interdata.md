# The powder diffractogram

:::{.callout-note appearance="minimal"} 
## Introduction
A powder diffractogram displays the scattered intensity versus the Bragg angle $(2 \theta)$. It contains a number of peaks (reflections). The peaks are characterized by their position, intensity and profile. The peaks and the background are the source of all information of teh X-ray powder diffraction technique. Table 1 shows an overview.
:::

:::{table} General survey of the information content of a powder diffractogram
:label: table
:align: center

| Reflection | depends on | main information
| --- | --- |---|
| position | - priodic arrangement of the atoms/molecules <br> -wavelength used | - qualitative phase analysis <br> - lattice parameters <br> residual stress |
| Intensity | - crystal structure <br> - wavelength used <br> - sample preparation | - quantitative phase analysis <br> crystal structure <br> - preferred orientations/texture |
| Profile | - lattice distortions | - micro-strain <br> - crystallite sizes |

:::
